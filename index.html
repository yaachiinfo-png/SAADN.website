About SAADN

The aim of SAADN is to fulfill the desires of Dietitians, Nutritionists and the Public. This will be done by providing Job opportunities to listed professionals, while meeting the nutritious status and well-being of the Public.

Dietitians and Nutritionists are facing high rate of unemployment, The public is facing a problem of malnutrition in the country. SAADN comes in as a solution to address the listed concerns. Solving the issue of unemployment is solving the issue of malnutrition.

These will be achieved by working alongside with the Government, existing dietetics organization, professionals and the public at large.

Our Vision & Mission

Vision

To create a South Africa where the Public enjoy optimal health and well-being through access to Dietitians and Nutritionists whom will provide evidence-based nutrition guidance, equitable food system, and a supportive environment that promotes healthy lifestyles for all.

Mission

To promote optimal nutrition and enhance the nutritional well-being of South Africans while advancing the profession of Dietetics and Nutrition in the country.

Our Objectives

Main Objectives

  1. Create employment for Dietitians and Nutritionists with the purpose of serving the Public to fulfil their nutritional health needs and maintaining the well-being of South Africans.
  2. To promote and maintain the professional integrity of ethical conduct and legitimate interests of Dietitians and Nutritionists at all levels and setting both in private and public sectors.

Secondary Objectives

  1. Advocating for recognition of our noble professions and the important role it plays in the healthcare system of South Africa.
  2. Negotiating with the Government for job creation in the health sector (hospitals, clinics) and promote for employment to be equally be accessible in all provinces.
  3. To play an active role in promoting healthy eating and provision of nutrition education in all platforms and settings to eradicate malnutrition in South Africa.
  4. To form part of all feeding schemes in the country, to promote accessible to not only food but nutritious food to the public.
  5. To develop and establish relationships among members and other health-associated groups.
  6. Advocating for quality education of Dietetics and Nutrition courses in all universities.
